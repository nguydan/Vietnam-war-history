About the Vietnam War

The Vietnam War was not a single, isolated conflict but a layered struggle shaped by colonialism, nationalism, Cold War ideology, and internal Vietnamese divisions. What began as a fight for independence from French colonial rule gradually evolved into one of the most complex and controversial wars of the twentieth century.

For the Vietnamese people, the conflict spanned decades and involved competing visions for the nationâ€™s future. For the United States and its allies, Vietnam became a focal point of global containment strategy against communism. These overlapping objectives transformed a regional struggle into an international war with far-reaching political, social, and human consequences.

Key Eras:

  • 1945-1954: The Anti-French Resistance and the birth of the DRV.
  • 1954-1964: The Partition and the rise of the Republic of Vietnam.
  • 1965-1975: American Intervention and the limits of conventional power.
